# PRD: PJ Site Services Landing Page

## Project Name

PJ Site Services

## Goal

Build a simple, clean, industrial-logistics style Next.js landing page for PJ Site Services, a new High Desert site services company.

The site should have two main experiences:

1. Public customer-facing landing page  
   A polished page that could be sent to real potential customers.

2. Private partner detail page/section for Peter  
   A more detailed internal concept page explaining the business idea, service lines, rollout plan, and why this is a good opportunity.

There is no backend, no database, no login, and no real quote form submission for now. This is an MVP/static website.

---

## Tech Stack

Use:

- Next.js
- TypeScript
- Tailwind CSS
- App Router
- Responsive mobile-first design

Keep it simple and easy to edit.

### Section 1: Why Not Just “PJ Storage”?

Explain:

The company should not be named only around storage because the long-term opportunity is broader than storing boats, RVs, trailers, and containers.

PJ Site Services allows the company to expand into:

- Outdoor storage
- Commercial cleaning
- Pressure washing
- Fleet washing
- Landscaping
- Irrigation
- Yard cleanup
- Site maintenance
- Contractor support
- Commercial property services

This keeps the brand flexible.

---

### Section 2: Core Business Idea

Title:

The Core Idea

Copy:

PJ Site Services starts as a simple High Desert site services company. The first wedge can be outdoor storage, boat/RV storage, container storage, pressure washing, or commercial cleaning. Over time, the company can add recurring service lines that use the same local customer base, the same operations mindset, and the same simple sales process.

The goal is not to build a complicated tech company. The goal is to build a boring local services company with better systems, better follow-up, better customer communication, and eventually better automation.

### Section 4: MVP Strategy

Title:

Simple MVP Plan

Create a numbered list:

1. Pick one simple High Desert service to validate first.
2. Build a basic landing page.
3. Contact boat/RV owners, contractors, property managers, warehouses, fleet operators, and commercial sites.
4. Try to get the first paying customer.
5. Keep the first version simple with no backend or complicated software.
6. Track every lead manually.
7. Learn what people actually want.
8. Add recurring services once demand is clear.

### Section 6: Why This Could Work

Title:

Why This Opportunity Makes Sense

Bullet points:

- High Desert land can be cheaper than coastal Southern California.
- Many customers need storage, cleaning, washing, and maintenance but do not want to coordinate multiple vendors.
- Boring services can create recurring revenue.
- The company can start very small.
- The brand can expand without needing a rebrand.
- Most small service businesses have weak websites, weak follow-up, and weak systems.
- AI and automation can help later with quoting, scheduling, customer follow-up, reminders, routing, and operations.
